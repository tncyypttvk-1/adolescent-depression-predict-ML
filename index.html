📊 Project Summary

This study investigates the effectiveness of internet penetration rates, per-capita GDP levels, and urbanization rates in explaining adolescent depression prevalence. It presents a comprehensive machine learning analysis using the Random Forest algorithm, based on data from 139 countries during 2000-2022.

🔍 Research Question

Do countries' internet penetration rates, per-capita GDP levels, and urbanisation rates significantly explain adolescent depression prevalence (%)?

✨ Key Findings

Optimal Algorithm

Random Forest demonstrated the best performance: CV RMSE = 0.1880, Test RMSE = 0.1923

Most Important Predictor

GDP per Capita (42.3% importance): Reflects advanced diagnostic capacity and record-keeping systems.

Secondary Feature

Internet Penetration (31.7% importance): Strong correlation between digital connectivity levels and depression reporting.

Third Predictor

Urbanization (22.1% importance): Urban density correlates with social isolation and mental health issues.

📋 Data & Methodology

📊 Data Sources

  • World Bank WDI: Economic and urbanization indicators
  • IHME GBD 2019: Global disease burden and health data
  • Time Period: 2000-2022 (23 years)
  • Geographic Scope: 139 countries

🔧 Methodology

  • Data Split: 75% training, 25% testing
  • Cross-Validation: 5-Fold CV
  • Algorithms: Linear Regression, Decision Tree, Random Forest
  • Optimization: Hyperparameter tuning (mtry, min_n)
